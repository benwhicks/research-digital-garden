Latin for "all else being equal". This is a [[dialogic\|dialogic]] version of [[statistical-control\|statistical-control]]. #latin

> [!note] From Wikipedia
> _**_Ceteris paribus_**_ (also spelled _**_caeteris paribus_**_) (Classical Latin pronunciation: [[ˈkeːtɛ.riːs ˈpa.rɪ.bʊs\|ˈkeːtɛ.riːs ˈpa.rɪ.bʊs]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Latin "Help:IPA/Latin")) is a [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin") phrase, meaning "other things equal"; some other English translations of the phrase are "**all other things being equal**", "**other things held constant**", "**all else unchanged**", and "**all else being equal**".[[1\|1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceteris_paribus#cite_note-1) A statement about a [causal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ontic "Ontic"), [empirical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epistemic "Epistemic"), [moral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morality "Morality"), or [logical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inductive_logic "Inductive logic") relation between two states of affairs is _ceteris paribus_ if it is acknowledged that the statement, although usually accurate in expected conditions, can fail because of, or the relation can be abolished by, intervening factors.
